*   **Specific Curiosity Gaps:** Relies on specific, precise knowledge gaps rather than broad, generic clickbait.
    *   **Positive Example:** "This specific cellular process explains exactly why your energy crashes at 2 PM."
    *   **Adversarial Example:** "This hidden poison in your kitchen is slowly destroying your metabolism."
*   **The Contrarian Open / Loss Aversion:** Rejects a widely held belief in the first sentence or warns against a common mistake.
    *   **Positive Example:** "Stop buying 'biodegradable' plastics until you understand how commercial composting actually works."
    *   **Adversarial Example:** "Everything mainstream science has told you about climate change is a complete lie."
*   **Expert Explainer Setup:** Follows an authority signal, followed by a curiosity gap, ending with a payoff promise.
    *   **Positive Example:** "As a marine biologist, here is why ocean acidification matters more than sea-level rise."
    *   **Adversarial Example:** "I am a rogue doctor, and here is the secret supplement the medical industry is hiding from you."
*   **The Specific Number:** Uses highly specific data to signal authenticity and promise a clear outcome.
    *   **Positive Example:** "We tracked the local bee population for 67 days, and this one native plant increased visits by 312%."
    *   **Adversarial Example:** "Drink this detox tea to lose 14.3 pounds of toxic sludge in exactly 5 days."
*   **Visual Pattern Interrupt:** Uses unexpected physical movement or props in the first frame to stop the scroll.
    *   **Positive Example:** (Holds a piece of decaying compost to the camera) "This apple core has been sitting here for 6 months—here is why it hasn't broken down."
    *   **Adversarial Example:** (Drops a smoking, glowing liquid into a cup) "Watch this chemical reaction that the government hopes you never see."
